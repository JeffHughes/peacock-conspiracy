# ITERATION 2 - CRITIQUE OF SUPER-BOOK v2

## What v2 Improved Over v1
1. **Disneyland thread is continuous** -- appears in Acts I, II, and III. The Shoppe is now a true mirror of the newsroom.
2. **Elena is fully human** -- her father's chapter (Ch 23) is potentially the novel's emotional climax.
3. **Dark humor works** -- "The Rachel" counter, Danny the screenwriter, Frank's food-based truth-bombs, Kato Kaelin.
4. **Probation subplot has teeth** -- starts as background color, becomes institutional weapon.
5. **Pop culture markers are specific** -- monthly cultural references ground every chapter.
6. **Marcus Webb has an arc** -- principled journalist who stays and finds a different path.
7. **The Friends taping scene** is one of the novel's best set pieces.
8. **Tupac's death** provides emotional counterweight and racial dimension.
9. **Richard Jewell encounter** is brief and devastating.
10. **30 chapters feel complete** -- the structure has no obvious gaps.

## Remaining Weaknesses

### 1. The Conspiracy Needs One More Layer
The evidence is good but needs a HUMAN COST moment. The narrator should discover that someone -- maybe the security consultant Denton -- was threatened or harmed. Not murdered (too thriller-cliche), but silenced in a way that shows institutional power at work. A hospitalization, a financial destruction, something.

### 2. The Election Integration is Still Thin
Clinton's re-election on Nov 5 is mentioned but not dramatized. The narrator should be at the KNBC election night desk, watching how TV calls elections in real-time. This foreshadows the 2000 Fox debacle.

### 3. Agent Whitfield Needs More Presence
He appears briefly in Atlanta but disappears. He should reappear in Act III -- perhaps contacting the narrator independently, having also found discrepancies in the Jewell case. Two men from opposite sides of the institutional fence, arriving at similar conclusions independently.

### 4. The Ending Could Be More Visceral
The flight to London is well-constructed but needs one more emotional beat. Perhaps the narrator calls Frank from the airport. Frank says: "You're doing the right thing. I should have done it thirty years ago."

### 5. Need More Newsroom Texture
The daily experience of working at a TV station -- the smells, the sounds, the exhaustion, the adrenaline rushes of breaking news. What it's like to be 19 and running tape at 2 AM.

### 6. The "Author's Note" Idea Was Mentioned But Not Written
A fictional author's note at the end could add a final layer of metafiction.

## Plan for Iteration 3
1. Add Denton's silencing as a human cost moment
2. Add election night scene at KNBC
3. Expand Whitfield's reappearance in Act III
4. Add the Frank phone call from the airport
5. Add more sensory newsroom detail
6. Write the fictional author's note
7. Final polish on all chapter summaries with specific scene details
8. Add sample prose passages for key scenes
