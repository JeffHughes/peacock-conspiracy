## REVISED CHAPTER OUTLINE (30 Chapters)

### ACT I: "THE DISPLAY" (Chapters 1-11)

**Chapter 1: "A Moveable Feast"** -- London frame. (Unchanged from v1.)

**Chapter 2: "The Earthquake Room"** -- USC arrival. Crack in ceiling. Bronco chase. Friends premiere in dorm. (Unchanged.)

**Chapter 3: "The Shoppe"** -- Castle Heraldry Shoppe deep dive. "The Rachel" observation. Metaphor established.
- NEW: Narrator describes the commute -- USC to Anaheim, 45 minutes if traffic cooperates (it never does). The 5 to the 91. AM radio. The transition from South LA to Orange County is a class migration.
- NEW: A coworker, DANNY, is an aspiring screenwriter who works the Shoppe and narrates his customers as movie characters. Comic relief throughout.

**Chapter 4: "Channel 4"** -- First day at KNBC. Assignment desk. Meets Frank. (Mostly unchanged.)
- NEW: Describes the Brokaw News Center hallways -- awards on walls, signed headshots, a faded photo of Tom Brokaw from his early days. The building smells like coffee and toner.
- NEW: Fritz Coleman (real weatherman) walks past and makes a joke about the rain forecast. Establishes the real people in the building.

**Chapter 5: "The Package"** -- News package construction. OJ coverage. Marcus Webb introduced. (Mostly unchanged.)
- NEW: Marcus's lead poisoning story expanded. He shot it himself, edited it himself, wrote it beautifully. It gets 45 seconds in the C-block between a car chase and a weather tease. He watches it air and his jaw tightens.
- NEW: Dark humor -- a PA accidentally airs 3 seconds of a reporter picking his nose during a live toss. The newsroom erupts in laughter. Frank: "That's the most honest thing we've broadcast all week."

**Chapter 6: "Probation"** -- NEW CHAPTER. The narrator's probation check-in.
- His probation officer is a tired woman named CAROL who has 200 cases and doesn't care about a kid who stole a book.
- But probation means: no arrests, no "conduct detrimental to rehabilitation," regular check-ins.
- The irony: he's on probation for stealing a book about fleeing to Paris while working for institutions built on manufactured truth.
- He tells Carol he's a student who works at Disneyland and a TV station. She says: "Keep your head down. Don't do anything stupid."
- Foreshadows: the probation becomes a weapon when the institution wants to silence him.

**Chapter 7: "The Verdict"** -- OJ verdict. Campus splits. (From v1 Ch 6.)
- NEW: That night, narrator goes to the Castle Heraldry Shoppe for a weekend shift. Disneyland is surreally normal. Families buying plastic swords and fake crests. Outside, America is ripping itself apart over race and justice. Inside the castle, it's always 1955.

**Chapter 8: "Elena"** -- Meets Elena. Dateline precedent. (From v1 Ch 7.)
- NEW: More specific USC texture. They study in the Info Commons -- the 24-hour computer lab in Leavey Library basement. Mosaic browser. AltaVista search engine. Elena shows him how to search NEXIS-LEXIS for old news articles. "This is how you find what they buried," she says.
- NEW: They see "Heat" (1995) at a theater on Figueroa. The scene where De Niro says "Don't let yourself get attached to anything you are not willing to walk out on in 30 seconds flat" -- they laugh. It won't be funny later.

**Chapter 9: "Sweeps"** -- November sweeps. Fear-based coverage. Diana Cross appears. (From v1 Ch 8.)
- NEW: During sweeps, the narrator works both jobs in the same day. 6 AM at KNBC. 4 PM drive to Anaheim. 5-11 PM at the Shoppe. Home to USC by midnight. Classes at 8 AM. He's running on Red Bull before Red Bull exists. (Jolt Cola.)
- NEW: At the Shoppe, Danny the screenwriter is writing a spec script about a newsroom conspiracy. Jake laughs. "Nobody would believe that."

**Chapter 10: "Must-See"** -- Friends taping. NBC's cultural power. $456M investment. (From v1 Ch 9.)
- NEW: **Expanded Friends taping scene.** The narrator gets a ticket through KNBC connections. Stage 24 at Warner Bros. (technically not NBC's stage, but the NBC connection gets him in).
  - The set is smaller than it looks on TV. Central Perk's couch is worn.
  - Jennifer Aniston walks to her mark. The audience gasps. She waves.
  - A scene is shot 4 times. Each take slightly different. The director picks the one with the biggest laugh.
  - Between takes, the cast retreats to their chairs behind the cameras. They are working.
  - "Thirty million people would watch this Thursday. They would see the version the director chose. The take that got the biggest laugh. That's all anyone ever sees: the best take."
  - He thinks about the newsroom. "At KNBC, we did the same thing. We shot reality from multiple angles and aired the take that got the best rating. The only difference was, at Friends, everyone knew it was fiction."
- NEW: Tupac's "All Eyez on Me" album drops (Feb 1996). It's everywhere at USC. East Coast-West Coast tension is real and present on campus.

**Chapter 11: "Departure"** -- Atlanta bound. Elena gives him Chomsky. Briefing packet. END OF ACT I. (From v1 Ch 10.)
- NEW: Last visit to the Castle Heraldry Shoppe before Atlanta. Danny: "Bring me back a story, man." He hangs his cast member badge on the hook. Walks out of the castle for what he thinks is a temporary goodbye.

**Chapter 16: "Marcus"** -- NEW CHAPTER. Marcus Webb's subplot.
- Marcus is in Atlanta too, covering the Olympics for KNBC's local segments.
- He's doing excellent work: a piece on the Atlanta Black community's relationship with the Olympics, the displacement of homeless populations for the Games, the tension between corporate spectacle and local reality.
- His pieces are good. Really good. They barely air. The bombing coverage swallows everything.
- Marcus to Jake: "You know what the irony is? I came here to cover a story about a city. Now all anyone wants is the bombing. A bomb gets more airtime than a million people. That's the math."

**Chapter 17: "88 Days"** -- Jewell destroyed by media. Back in LA. Archives. Castle Heraldry Shoppe weekend. (From v1 Ch 16.)

- The parallel hits him: Richard Jewell's "profile" was something close. Not proof. An approximation. And the FBI let America believe.

**Chapter 18: "The Consultant"** -- Denton interview. Camera-to-vulnerability connection. (From v1 Ch 17.)

- Jake to Elena: "Tupac wrote about the system that's killing his community. Dole and Clinton are both part of that system. And the news covers both stories the same way: as entertainment."

**Chapter 20: "Fox"** -- Fox News launch. Jewell cleared. (From v1 Ch 18.)

### ACT III: "THE FLIGHT" (Chapters 23-30)

**Chapter 23: "Elena's Father"** -- NEW CHAPTER. Elena's perspective (reconstructed by narrator).
- The narrator tells us Elena's father's story in full, as she told it to him over months.
- David Marsh. Hartford Courant. Features writer. Good at his job.
- He fabricated quotes in a homelessness series because the real homeless people he interviewed were inarticulate and the story was important. He made the quotes better. More eloquent. More moving.
- "He didn't lie about the truth. He improved the truth's packaging. And the system destroyed him for it. Meanwhile, the network that blew up a truck on camera -- that network made a hundred million dollars at the Olympics."
- David died alone. Age 47. Heart attack in a studio apartment filled with newspapers.
- Elena: "My father was the sacrifice. The system needed someone to punish so it could pretend the problem was one bad person and not the whole machine."
- This is why Elena understands the narrator's situation. This is why she says: run.

**Chapter 24: "What I Knew and What I Could Prove"** -- Devastating inventory. (From v1 Ch 21.)
- NEW: Expanded with the narrator returning to the Castle Heraldry Shoppe one more time. He sits behind the counter after closing. The castle is empty. The lights are on but nobody's there.
- He thinks about every crest he sold. Every family that walked out believing something that wasn't true. "I was a very good liar. But I only lied about things that didn't matter. The people I worked for lied about things that destroyed lives."

**Chapter 25: "The Envelope"** -- The threat arrives. Probation weaponized. Break-in. (From v1 Ch 22.)
- NEW: The probation weapon is made explicit. His PO Carol calls: "Someone from a corporate security firm reached out to us expressing concerns about your mental health and behavior. That's... unusual. What's going on?" The narrator realizes: they're using his probation as leverage. If his PO recommends revocation, he goes to jail.

**Chapter 26: "The Whistleblower's Graveyard"** -- Research on Webb, Wigand, Silkwood. Elena's LSE acceptance. (From v1 Ch 23.)

**Chapter 28: "Flight"** -- LAX to London. (From v1 Ch 25.)
- NEW: On the plane, he reads the in-flight magazine. There's a profile of Dick Ebersol and NBC's Olympic coverage. The headline: "The Triumph of the Peacock." Jake closes the magazine and puts it in the seat-back pocket. He never reads another piece of NBC content again.

**Chapter 29: "November 2000 / The Peacock Conspiracy"** -- Combines v1 Ch 26-27.

## NEW ELEMENTS IN v2

### Running Gags / Dark Humor
1. **"The Rachel" counter:** The narrator keeps an informal count of how many women with "The Rachel" haircut he encounters. It starts at the Shoppe ("Rachel #47 bought a Scottish crest") and continues through the novel. By the time he leaves for London, he's in the hundreds. Last one is a flight attendant on the transatlantic flight.
2. **Danny the screenwriter:** Disneyland coworker provides comic relief and meta-commentary. He's writing a newsroom conspiracy spec script that mirrors the real events. Jake can never tell if Danny is genius or insane.
3. **Kato Kaelin jokes:** Running references to Kato's absurd celebrity. At one point, Kato appears at a KNBC affiliate event. Frank: "There he is. The most famous unemployed houseguest in America. We created him. He's our Frankenstein."
4. **Newsroom food:** Frank always eats while delivering devastating truths. This becomes a running pattern. Turkey sandwich: "The news isn't what happened." Burrito: "Sweeps is when we stop pretending." Pizza: "You can't fight the system from inside."

### Deepened Pop Culture Integration
- **Monthly markers:** Each chapter references specific cultural moments to ground the timeline
  - Ch 2 (Fall 94): Pulp Fiction in theaters, Kurt Cobain tribute on MTV
  - Ch 5 (Spring 95): Notorious B.I.G.'s "Ready to Die" playing in cars outside KNBC
  - Ch 7 (Oct 95): "Toy Story" trailer, Windows 95 on every computer
  - Ch 8 (Fall 95): Alanis Morissette's "Jagged Little Pill" on the drive to Anaheim
  - Ch 10 (Early 96): "Heat" conversation with Elena. Tupac's "All Eyez on Me"
  - Ch 12 (Jul 96): "Independence Day" in theaters. Ironic: a movie about fighting alien invaders while real Americans can't fight their own institutions
  - Ch 19 (Sep 96): Tupac dies. Fugees' "The Score" at USC parties.
  - Ch 20 (Oct 96): First episode of Fox News. "Jerry Maguire" promotional posters. "Show me the money" as a newsroom catchphrase.

### Enhanced Probation Subplot
- Ch 6: Probation established. Carol the PO. "Don't do anything stupid."
- Ch 9: Probation check-in while working 20-hour days. Carol: "You look terrible. Are you eating?"
- Ch 17: Probation mentioned as narrator considers his options. Can't get arrested. Can't make waves.
- Ch 25: Probation weaponized. Corporate security contacts PO. The system's leverage point.
- Ch 28: On the plane to London, narrator realizes: by leaving the country, he's technically violating probation. He doesn't care. He's already being punished for something he didn't do. Why not add running?

## REVISED CONSPIRACY EVIDENCE CHAIN

| Chapter | Evidence | Innocent Explanation | Suspicious Reading |
|---------|----------|---------------------|-------------------|
| 10 | Unusually detailed contingency protocols | Standard event planning | Pre-planned exploitation |
| 13 | Cameras pre-positioned at blast site | Cameras everywhere at Olympics | Specific targeting |
| 14 | Timestamp: camera moved 20 min before blast | Routine repositioning | Foreknowledge |
| 15 | "Priority zones" email, 6hrs pre-bombing | Security awareness | Advance notice |
| 17 | Off-calendar meetings, unexplained expenses | Normal corporate complexity | Hidden coordination |
| 18 | Fired consultant confirms NBC knew vulnerabilities | NBC was informed but didn't act | NBC chose not to act |
| 21 | Frank's GE/family protection pattern | Corporate self-interest is normal | Institutional cover-up culture |
| 22 | Diana's "that's professionalism not conspiracy" | She may be right | She's been trained to say that |

**KEY PRINCIPLE:** Every piece of evidence has a plausible innocent explanation. The conspiracy is in the PATTERN, not any single fact. The narrator sees a pattern. The reader must decide if the pattern is real or imposed.

## CHARACTER ARCS (EXPANDED)

### Narrator's Arc
Naive believer -> Excited learner -> Complicit participant -> Shocked witness -> Determined investigator -> Paranoid target -> Wise exile

### Elena's Arc
Controlled survivor -> Intellectual challenger -> Research partner -> Frightened protector -> Decisive rescuer
(Her arc mirrors her father's in reverse: where David Marsh was destroyed by the system, Elena escapes it)

### Frank's Arc
Cynical survivor -> Reluctant mentor -> Protective father figure -> Terrified truth-teller -> Left behind
(Frank's tragedy: he stays. He can't leave. He's too old, too embedded, too alcoholic. He watches Jake go and knows he should have gone thirty years ago.)

### Marcus Webb's Arc
Principled idealist -> Frustrated craftsman -> Strategic compromiser -> Eventual escapee
(In the epilogue, we learn Marcus left TV for print and won a Pulitzer. He found a way to stay in journalism by leaving television.)

### Diana Cross's Arc
Competent corporate warrior -> Gracious recruiter -> Cold pragmatist -> (Ambiguous) protector or threat
(We never know if Diana is complicit or simply competent. In the epilogue, she's been promoted. The system rewards her either way.)
